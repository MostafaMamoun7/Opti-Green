Project Description

Our project is a solar-powered “smart home” system, which uses advanced solar financing and sun tracking technologies, along with smart security and lighting features. The project aims to provide a basic, safe, and energy-efficient home, resulting in a user experience that is effective technical solutions for modern homes.

Project Modules

Tracking solar panel

Components Used in This Module

Solar panels

Used to capture sunlight and convert it into electrical energy. This energy can be used to power the system or charge the battery for storage and later use.

Arduino UNO

It serves as the "brain" of the system. The microcontroller on the Arduino UNO board reads data from the LDRs (light-dependent resistors) and analyzes the direction of the incoming sunlight. Based on this analysis, it controls the servo motors to adjust the angle of the solar panels for optimal sunlight exposure.

LDR

They are used to measure the intensity of the light falling on them. In the solar tracking system, the LDRs are placed on both sides (right and left) of the solar panel to measure the difference in light intensity. Based on these measurements, the direction in which the solar panels should move to capture the maximum amount of light is determined.

Resistors

They are used in the circuit with the LDRs to form a voltage divider. This allows the changes in resistance to be converted into voltage values that can be read by the Arduino, enabling precise control of the solar panels' direction based on the light level.

Batteries

They are used to store the electrical energy collected from the solar panels. The batteries can supply power to the system when needed, such as operating the servo motors to adjust the panels or powering the system during the night when there is no solar energy.

Servo motor (SG90)

It is used to precisely adjust the angle of the solar panels. Based on the LDR data readings, the servo motor moves to position the solar panels in the optimal direction to capture the maximum amount of light

Lithium battery charging circuit

Battery Protection When the battery reaches the specified voltage (4.2 volts), the protection circuit in the charger automatically disconnects the charging. This protection prevents overcharging, which could damage the battery or even pose a risk of explosion. The protection circuit also monitors the battery to prevent deep discharge, disconnecting the battery if its voltage drops to extremely low levels.

IC(LM2596) DC-DC

By using the LM2596 in the project, it ensures that the Arduino receives the appropriate voltage (5V) from the battery (8V), allowing the system to operate continuously and efficiently without the need for another power source. .

-Night Lighting Poles

Components Used in This Module

LDR

Capturing the sunlight signal.

Resistors

Transistor

The control of the signal between the light-dependent resistor (LDR) and the regular resistor: when the LDR detects light, the transistor disconnects the circuit.

Garage Light Sensor

Components Used in This Module

IR Sensor

It is a device that uses infrared rays to detect objects or motion between the transmitter and receiver.

LED

The LED lights up when the sensor detects the car entering the garage.

Car

When the car enters the garage, the IR sensor detects it and lights up the garage.

Remote light control by (IR-receiver)

Components Used in This Module

IC 555

The IC 555 is used as a latch or pulse generator (Monostable Mode) to operate an LED for a specific duration or until it is reset.

Capacitor

Storing the charge for a short period of time and determining the duration for which the output (Pin 3) of the IC 555 remains high.

IR-Receiver

It is a sensor designed to receive infrared (IR) signals sent from the remote control.

LED

It visually displays the circuit's operating status. It lights up when receiving the "ON" signal from the remote control.

Resistance

It is used to determine the current or voltage in the circuit and protect it from damage.

Remote control

When you press a button on the remote, an infrared light-emitting diode (IR LED) is activated to send an invisible light signal to the human eye.
